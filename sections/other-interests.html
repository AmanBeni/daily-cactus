A university banned a human sexuality textbook because one student called it pornography

The University of Nebraska at Kearney has removed a standard textbook on human sexuality from a course after a single student's complaint that it constituted "pornography." The move is part of a broader US pattern of academic curriculum challenges that has accelerated since 2022, targeting subjects from sexuality to critical race theory.

Why it matters: One data point in a pattern: academic censorship triggered by individual complaints, without systematic review, is becoming normalised in American higher education. Worth knowing as context for global academic freedom debates — including in India, where curriculum disputes follow their own political logic.
