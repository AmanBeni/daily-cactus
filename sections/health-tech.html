US Infant Mortality Falls to All-Time Low — But Still Trails Peer Nations

CDC data shows US infant mortality dropped to a new all-time low in 2025, driven by improvements in neonatal care, maternal health access, and preterm birth prevention. However, the US rate still significantly lags comparable high-income nations including Germany, France, Japan, and Canada — a gap that widens sharply along racial and geographic lines.

Key takeaway: The US is improving in absolute terms but falling further behind peers in relative terms — a healthcare paradox that points to systemic inequity rather than overall capability.

Why it matters: India's infant mortality rate, while much higher in absolute terms, has been declining faster than the US in percentage terms. The comparison underscores that healthcare access — not just technology — determines outcomes. India's ASHA programme and PM-JAY are the relevant levers, not hospital-grade AI tools.

Verge Labs Turns Failed Neurology Trial Data Into an AI Patient Stratification Model

Verge Labs has built an AI model that mines data from failed clinical trials — previously discarded as commercially worthless — to identify patient subpopulations that actually responded to treatments. The company's first model, trained on a failed ALS trial dataset, has already improved trial design for two new neurology programs by identifying biomarker-defined responder groups.

Key takeaway: Clinical trial failure is a data goldmine if you have the AI to read it — and Verge Labs has found the key.

Why it matters: India generates substantial clinical trial data through CROs like Parexel and IQVIA's Indian operations. Indian biotech startups could apply similar approaches to India-specific disease datasets, particularly for TB, diabetes complications, and infectious disease trials that are underrepresented globally.

Medicare Covers Weight-Loss Drugs Starting July — a Temporary Fix That May Prove Permanent

Medicare will begin covering GLP-1 obesity drugs including semaglutide starting July 2026 under a limited demonstration programme. Industry analysts expect the programme — marketed as temporary — to become effectively permanent due to the sheer number of beneficiaries and the political difficulty of withdrawing a popular drug benefit once granted.

Key takeaway: GLP-1 coverage for Medicare is a one-way door — once opened, politically impossible to close.

Why it matters: GLP-1 drugs are entering the Indian market at premium pricing. If Medicare legitimises broad coverage, it accelerates global availability and may bring down prices over the next 3-5 years, making them accessible to India's large Type 2 diabetes population earlier than currently projected.

Congress Must Reauthorize the Pandemic Preparedness Act Before America Is Caught Flat-Footed Again

The Pandemic and All-Hazards Preparedness Act — which funds the US medical countermeasures enterprise, stockpile management, and surge hospital capacity — is due for reauthorization with Congress distracted by other priorities. Former officials warn that failure to reauthorize would hollow out pandemic preparedness infrastructure built after COVID-19.

Key takeaway: The political will to fund pandemic preparedness always evaporates between crises — and the US is heading toward another gap.

Why it matters: India's pandemic preparedness hinges partly on global health infrastructure, including WHO and US-funded surveillance networks. A hollowed-out US preparedness system reduces global early-warning capacity for the next outbreak.
