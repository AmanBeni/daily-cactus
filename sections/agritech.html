A startup says AI-optimised plasma can make green ammonia nearly as cheap as fossil-fuel fertiliser

Faraday Earth claims its AI-tuned plasma reactor can produce green ammonia at around $500 per ton — within striking distance of conventional gray ammonia's cost of production. The Haber-Bosch process, which the startup aims to displace, currently accounts for roughly 2% of global CO₂ emissions and is deeply embedded in global food systems.

Why it matters: India is one of the world's largest fertiliser importers and consumers, and its agricultural subsidy bill is heavily influenced by global urea prices. Affordable green ammonia would have large implications for Indian farm input costs, subsidy bills, and climate commitments in one move.

Rainbow Crops raises $11M to use AI for editing multiple genes in crops simultaneously

Rainbow Crops has secured $11.25 million to advance its AI-guided multiplex gene editing platform, which edits multiple genes in crops simultaneously to improve yield, resilience, and nutritional profiles. A corn collaboration with a major seed company is reportedly close to announcement, with additional crop partnerships in discussion.

Why it matters: Multiplex gene editing bypasses the one-gene-at-a-time bottleneck of conventional crop biotech, making it directly relevant to complex traits like drought tolerance and heat resilience — exactly the characteristics Indian agriculture needs as climate variability intensifies.

Picketa Systems bets real-time soil and leaf data can close the fertiliser loop

Picketa Systems has raised $1.5 million to commercialise a portable field-testing device for crop nutrient status, enabling farmers to make real-time fertiliser decisions rather than relying on slow lab tests. CEO Xavier Hebert-Couturier says autonomous vehicles and precision inputs will close the fertiliser loop within a decade.

Why it matters: Over-fertilisation is a major cost and environmental problem in Indian agriculture. A device that gives granular data at the plant level — rather than the field level — could materially reduce input costs and the runoff that is degrading India's groundwater.

French alt-meat startup SWAP Food collapses after raising €100M+ — the sector's shakeout continues

SWAP Food, a Paris-based company specialising in whole-cut plant-based chicken fillets, has entered judicial liquidation after failing to reach profitability despite raising over €100 million since 2020. Technically impressive products couldn't compete on taste, price, or distribution against conventional meat in a post-hype market.

Why it matters: The alt-meat sector's collapse of well-funded darlings continues. For Indian agritech founders targeting alternative protein, this is hard data: technical innovation plus clever capital raising is not enough without a clear path to commercial competitiveness. The graveyard is well-populated now.
