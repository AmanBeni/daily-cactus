No jailbreak needed: Fable 5 generated dangerous code output from a routine developer prompt

A security researcher found that Anthropic's Fable 5 model produced code with serious security implications when given a simple "fix this code" instruction — no jailbreak, no adversarial prompt engineering required. US federal officials were alarmed enough to treat it as a red-flag safety event, according to The Register. The researcher stressed this came from a standard development workflow, not a stress test.

Why it matters: If frontier AI can be steered toward harmful outputs through ordinary dev tasks, the "responsible deployment" frameworks that enterprises, investors, and regulators rely on need rethinking from first principles. For anyone advising on AI adoption or building AI-integrated products, this belongs in your next risk assessment — not your footnotes.

Better ACs could save Indian households ₹69 billion a year, analysis finds

A Carbon Brief analysis finds that switching to higher-efficiency air-conditioning units across India could collectively save households ₹69 billion ($724 million) annually. With AC ownership expanding rapidly amid heatwaves, the gap between cheap and premium models is generating a hidden affordability trap: people buy cheap, then pay more on electricity over the appliance's lifetime.

Why it matters: India's cooling load is one of the largest forces shaping future grid demand. This is both a market signal and a governance challenge — appliance efficiency standards, not just market forces, will determine which direction India's power bill and carbon trajectory goes. For climate tech founders and energy investors, the addressable market here is large and the policy lever is within reach.

Why it matters: Founder-era exits at Tata-backed platforms show how India's largest conglomerate is professionalising its digital bets. The quick-commerce war is now a fight between professional operators with institutional capital — the founder-scrappy phase is over. Watch how Nanda positions BigBasket's differentiation against 10-minute delivery players.
