Someone built a banned book library inside a smart light bulb's Wi-Fi captive portal

Richard Osgood has published a write-up of a project in which he loaded a library of banned books into the Wi-Fi captive portal of a smart light bulb — so anyone who tries to connect to the bulb's network gets served a complete banned book. The project requires no internet connection, leaves no obvious trace, and is deployed in a device people rarely think twice about.

Why it matters: Pure delight. Also a reminder that distributed information resilience doesn't require sophisticated infrastructure — just creative use of what already exists. If you're building products at the intersection of connectivity and information access, this is a charming philosophical provocation about where data can live.
