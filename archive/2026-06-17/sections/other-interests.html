KIN by Tayari Jones is the most talked-about novel of 2026 so far

Book Riot confirms that KIN by Tayari Jones has topped virtually every "best books of 2026" list to date, making it the consensus literary standout of the year's first half. Jones, known for An American Marriage, brings her characteristic focus on family fracture and social context — this time in what's being described as a multigenerational reckoning.

Why it matters: If you read one novel from 2026, this appears to be the early frontrunner for the kind that prompts real conversation. Worth a look, or at minimum, worth knowing what the cultural discussion is about.

A US university has banned a human sexuality textbook because a single student complained it was pornographic

The University of Nebraska at Kearney has removed a standard academic textbook on human sexuality from a course after one student complained that it constituted pornography. The university administration sided with the complaint and banned the text, prompting criticism from academic freedom advocates.

Why it matters: The pattern — a single complaint triggering institutional capitulation — is a feature, not a bug, of the current US culture-war dynamic in higher education. The direction of US academic freedom has implications for anyone tracking how educational institutions globally are being reshaped by political pressure, including in India.

A24 is adapting Jeffrey Eugenides' The Marriage Plot, with Sadie Sink attached to star

A24 has announced a film adaptation of Jeffrey Eugenides' 2011 literary novel The Marriage Plot, with Stranger Things star Sadie Sink attached to lead. The novel — a campus story about English literature students navigating ideas and each other in the 1980s — has been a favourite of literary readers since publication.

Why it matters: A24 adapting a beloved literary novel is culturally interesting in itself, but the studio's track record of making literary fiction accessible without neutering it makes this one to watch. Worth flagging for the intersection of books and prestige film culture.
