Nasscom Showcases 12 Agri- and Water-Tech Startups Moving from Pilots to Enterprise Scale

At its Deeptech Confluence 2026 event in New Delhi, Nasscom's DTC Accelerate program highlighted 12 startups from a 20-week growth programme, working across AI-enabled water intelligence, precision agriculture, soil health, aquaculture, decentralized bio-manufacturing, and farm traceability. The event's stated signal: India's agri-water deeptech ecosystem is moving from 100-farmer pilots toward enterprise-scale deployments and is attracting serious attention from corporates, investors, and policymakers.

Why it matters: The transition from "impressive pilot" to "revenue-generating enterprise contract" is the graveyard most agritech startups never cross. Nasscom's corporate network and platform are specifically designed to compress this timeline. Worth tracking which of these 12 ventures announce their first major enterprise deployments over the next 6 months.

India Agritech on Track for $24 Billion in Cumulative Funding; Drone Agriculture Expands Nationwide

India's agritech sector is on course for cumulative funding to reach $24 billion by end-2026, with more than 70% of Indian farmers expected to be using some form of digital planning tool. Robotic harvesters and smart irrigation systems are entering pilot phases across Punjab, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu. The government's Drone Didi scheme is extending drone access and training to women farmers across rural India.

Why it matters: These headline funding numbers often conceal thin unit economics and pilot-stage overstatement — a word of caution is always warranted in agritech. But the combination of government subsidy infrastructure (drones), real climate pressure on yields, and digital public infrastructure is creating a genuine deployment window in the next 18–24 months. The question is which models convert at the unit economics level.
