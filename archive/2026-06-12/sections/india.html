RBI Holds Repo Rate at 5.25%, Cuts Growth Forecast to 6.6%, Raises Inflation Outlook — "Stagflation Risk" Now Named

The Monetary Policy Committee kept the repo rate unchanged at 5.25% for the second consecutive meeting, revised FY27 GDP growth down to 6.6% (from 6.9%), and raised the inflation projection to 5.1% (from 4.6%). Elevated global energy prices from the Iran-Hormuz conflict and supply chain disruptions are cited as the primary spillover risks driving both revisions.

Why it matters: The word "stagflation" is now appearing in mainstream market commentary about India. That term is unusual in an Indian context and signals that the RBI is navigating a genuine policy bind — cutting rates would risk inflation, while holding them risks growth. For anyone building or advising businesses in India, this changes the FY27 planning baseline in a meaningful way.

Three Indian Sailors Killed Off Oman in Third Ship Attack Linked to the Iran Conflict This Week

India confirmed the deaths of three Indian crew members in an incident involving a vessel near Shinas port off Oman's coast on June 11 — described as separate from two other attacks earlier this week in the same region. The attacks are linked to Iran's blockade of the Strait of Hormuz and the ongoing US-Iran conflict that began in February 2026.

Why it matters: India has the world's largest merchant seafarer workforce — hundreds of thousands of Indians crew commercial vessels globally. Ship attacks in an Indian Ocean-adjacent zone are not an abstract geopolitical issue; they are an immediate occupational safety and diplomatic crisis. Expect pressure on the government to seek guarantees for Indian seafarers and review maritime risk protocols.

India's Formal Flexi-Staffing Sector Grew 8% in FY26, Adding Over 1.18 Lakh Formal Workers

India's flexi-staffing industry recorded 8% year-on-year growth in new formal employment during FY26, adding 1.18 lakh organized-sector workers — primarily entry-level and blue-collar employees who would otherwise remain in the informal economy. The sector continues to act as a gateway to formal employment credentials, social security access, and documented work history.

Why it matters: Formal employment formation is one of India's most persistent structural challenges. An 8% growth rate in organized temporary work, while not spectacular, represents real formalisation at scale. Given that the economy also faces rising inflation and slower growth this year, tracking this metric matters — a drop here would be an early signal of household income stress.
