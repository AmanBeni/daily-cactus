India to Hit 300 GW of Non-Fossil Power by End of Quarter — Five Years Ahead of Schedule

Union Minister Pralhad Joshi announced India will cross 300 GW of installed non-fossil fuel capacity before the end of the current quarter. India also launched a Green Hydrogen Certification Portal to bring transparency to its nascent hydrogen supply chain.

Key takeaway: India is hitting its original 2030 non-fossil fuel target five years early and is now building the credentialing infrastructure to export green hydrogen.

Why it matters: 300 GW reframes India’s energy security story while the Iran-Hormuz shock is still fresh. The Green Hydrogen Portal is India’s bid to become a future exporter — a market the EU-India FTA could directly accelerate.

Invenergy: Trump’s Offshore Wind Payout Will Fund a Geothermal Push

Energy developer Invenergy is joining America’s race to build next-generation geothermal — funded by the controversial refund the Trump administration gave it for abandoning offshore wind projects. Geothermal is emerging as the favoured “always-on” clean energy bet of the Trump era.

Key takeaway: Trump’s war on offshore wind is inadvertently accelerating geothermal development as developers redirect capital.

Why it matters: India has significant geothermal potential (Ladakh, Himachal) that remains largely unexplored. Geothermal becoming cost-competitive in the US may accelerate Indian interest in the technology.

The Ocean Has Shielded Us From the Worst of Climate Change. Now It Is Running a Fever

Nearly every indicator of climate change is flashing red. In 2025, the number of days of marine heat wave conditions hit a record. Oceanographer Karina Von Schuckmann writes that the ocean’s absorptive capacity — which has muted warming for decades — is approaching its limits.

Key takeaway: The ocean heat buffer is saturating — meaning future warming will translate more directly into surface temperature and extreme weather events.

Why it matters: For India, the Indian Ocean’s temperature directly drives monsoon behaviour. A warming Indian Ocean is not an abstract climate risk — it’s a food security and agricultural planning issue.

US Public Still Favours Climate Action Despite Trump’s Fossil Fuel Drive

Two-thirds of Americans say they are worried about climate change despite the Trump administration’s frontal assault on climate policy and the media’s reduced coverage. The disconnect between elite politics and public opinion on climate is widening.

Key takeaway: US public support for climate action remains at ~65% despite two years of political signals in the opposite direction.

Why it matters: This is relevant to any Indian company considering a US market entry — demand for climate-aligned products and services remains robust despite the political environment.

Atlantic Hurricane Season Opens with Tropical Storm Arthur Inundating Gulf Coast

The first tropical storm of the 2026 Atlantic hurricane season formed and moved quickly into the Gulf coast, bringing dangerous flash flooding. It is the earliest named Atlantic storm since records began, arriving as warm ocean temperatures set seasonal records.

Key takeaway: The 2026 Atlantic hurricane season has opened at record pace, consistent with warming-driven predictions of earlier and more intense storm seasons.

Why it matters: Earlier, more intense storm seasons increase insurance losses and supply chain disruption globally. India’s growing reinsurance sector and coastal infrastructure both need to price this risk.
