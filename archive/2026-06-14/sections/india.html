Modi and Macron Open Bharat Innovates 2026 Today in Nice — 120 Indian Innovators, 500+ Investors

Prime Minister Modi and French President Macron are jointly inaugurating Bharat Innovates 2026 at the Palais des Expositions in Nice today (June 14), the centrepiece of the India-France Year of Innovation. The event brings 120 Indian deep tech ventures, ~15 higher education institutions, and 500+ investors across 13 frontier sectors including semiconductors, space technology, biotechnology, healthcare, advanced computing, and agri-tech. EAM Jaishankar and Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal accompany Modi. Earlier: India was confirmed as the Official AI Country Partner at VivaTech Paris (June 18), where Modi will present India's MANAV AI governance framework; the India-France Year of Innovation was inaugurated in Mumbai in February 2026.

Why it matters: Bharat Innovates is the most consequential positioning move India has made in deep tech diplomacy — explicitly framing India as a deep tech innovator, not just a software outsourcer or a large market. Watch for the bilateral MoUs announced this week: they typically define the research collaborations and joint venture funding pipelines of the next 2–3 years. The Indo-French technology corridor is deepening at exactly the moment India needs credible non-US, non-Chinese AI and deep tech partnerships.

India's Carbon Market Goes Binding: 490 Industrial Plants Now Have Mandatory GHG Emission Targets

India's Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS) now has legally binding compliance obligations in force for approximately 490 entities across seven energy-intensive sectors: aluminium, cement, chlor-alkali, pulp and paper, petroleum refining, petrochemicals, and textiles. Covered entities must meet GHG emission intensity targets (baseline: FY 2023-24); outperformers earn tradeable Carbon Credit Certificates (CCCs) on power exchanges; underperformers must buy and surrender CCCs. First credits are expected October 2026, with trading running November 2026 through January 2027 and repeating annually.

Why it matters: India's carbon market has moved from announcement to operational reality — a structural shift for every energy-intensive industrial business in the country. The next phase is expected to include steel and fertiliser. For strategy roles or investors across Indian industry, CBAM exposure on EU exports and domestic CCTS compliance are now concurrent planning inputs. The timeline to first trading (November 2026) is less than six months away.
