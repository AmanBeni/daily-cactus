Alibaba's Qwen 3.7 Max Runs 35 Hours Autonomously — and Costs 6× Less Than Claude Opus

Qwen 3.7 Max (released May 20 at Alibaba Cloud Summit) completed a verified 35-hour autonomous kernel-optimization run with 1,158 tool calls and a 10× speedup — the longest documented agentic run published. It scores 92.4 on GPQA Diamond, edging Claude Opus 4.6 Max (91.3), with a 1M-token context window. Price: $2.50 per million input tokens, roughly six times cheaper than Claude Opus 4.7. Why it matters: Frontier agentic capability at mid-tier cost changes the economics of AI-native products. The 35-hour autonomous run is a capability benchmark, not just a marketing stat.

Colorado Scraps Its Landmark AI Law Before It Ever Took Effect

Colorado Governor Jared Polis signed SB 26-189 on May 14, 2026, repealing and replacing the 2024 Colorado AI Act that was scheduled to take effect June 30. The replacement law, effective January 1, 2027, drops mandatory annual impact assessments and risk management programmes in favour of consumer disclosures, transparency requirements, human review rights, vendor accountability, and explainability. The engineering-process mandate model did not survive contact with industry lobbying.

Why it matters: The first comprehensive US state AI law was rolled back before a single enforcement action — a clear signal that the AI governance consensus is still forming and industry pushback works. The new disclosure-and-transparency model may become the US state-level template, notably lighter than the EU AI Act's tiered obligations. Earlier: the original 2024 Colorado Act was positioned as the first US state AI liability law.

A World Economic Forum piece on India's AI-for-public-good approach highlights the IndiaAI Mission (₹10,371.92 crore over five years), the AIKosh platform (7,541 datasets, 273 AI models across 20 sectors), and India's position as the world's third-largest startup ecosystem with 90%+ of startups using AI.
