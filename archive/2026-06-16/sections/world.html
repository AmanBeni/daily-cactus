Trump's Iran ceasefire reopens Hormuz — and leaves Netanyahu politically cornered

Donald Trump has brokered a ceasefire with Iran that re-opens the Strait of Hormuz after months of blockade. The deal places Israeli PM Netanyahu in a political trap: his far-right coalition partners oppose any arrangement that leaves Iran's nuclear programme intact, while Washington is pressing Israel to accept the terms. Netanyahu faces a lose-lose choice between destroying his coalition or defying the US.

Why it matters: The Hormuz re-opening matters directly to India — 85% of its oil passes through the strait. Whether the ceasefire holds, and what Iran gets in return, will determine the medium-term oil market trajectory. The India-stagflation-risk thread isn't resolved, but the direction of travel has improved.

Hungary bars Orbán from returning as prime minister — a constitutional milestone

Hungary's parliament has passed legislation limiting prime ministerial tenure to eight years, fulfilling a key promise of new PM Péter Magyar. The measure effectively bars Viktor Orbán — who held the office for over 14 years across two stints — from returning to power. It is the most consequential institutional change in Hungary in a decade.

Why it matters: Orbán's departure is significant for European politics — he was the EU's most prominent democratic backslider. The reset opens possibilities for Hungary's re-integration into EU mainstream, affecting the Poland-Hungary Eurosceptic axis and broader Central European dynamics.

US Air Force B-52 crashes in California — all 8 crew members killed

A US Air Force B-52 Stratofortress bomber crashed in California on Monday morning during a routine test mission, killing all eight crew members. It is the worst US military aircraft accident in recent years. An investigation is underway; the B-52 fleet, many aircraft over 60 years old, has been under extended modernisation scrutiny.

Why it matters: Context only — no direct India angle. The B-52 fleet's age and replacement costs are a significant line item in US defence budget negotiations that affect overall US military posture globally.
