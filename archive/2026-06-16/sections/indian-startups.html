Meesho Acquires Kirana Club for Rs 202 Cr to Build Out B2B Grocery Supply Chain

Meesho approved the all-cash acquisition of Kirana Club (Singapore-registered) and its subsidiary Retail Pulse Labs for Rs 202 crore, structured in three tranches with completion targeted by March 2027. Kirana Club connects 4.1 million registered kirana stores with FMCG brands and distributors across Tier 2-4 and rural India. Founded 2020 by Anshul Gupta and Aishwarya Jain; Kirana Club becomes a wholly-owned Meesho subsidiary on completion.

Why it matters: Meesho built its name in social commerce but this deal is a pivot to supply chain infrastructure — targeting the kirana economy that traditional e-commerce has never cracked. It's a real hypothesis test for B2B rural commerce at scale, and worth watching as a potential blueprint for how Indian internet companies monetize beyond metros.

Ather Energy Raises Rs 2,500 Cr Post-IPO to Scale Its Third EV Factory

Ather Energy's board approved a ₹2,500 crore fundraise — ₹1,500 Cr via QIP and ₹1,000 Cr via rights issue or FCCBs — its first capital raise since its April 2025 IPO. Funds will support Factory 3.0 in Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, targeting commercial operations by October 2026 with eventual capacity of 1.42 million units per year. Ather's market share rose from ~8% to 18.6% in Q4 FY26, driven by the Rizta scooter family.

Why it matters: A company doubling its EV market share in a year and immediately returning to markets for capacity capital is a strong signal of real demand. Ather is positioning itself as the premium-to-mainstream bridge in Indian EVs — Factory 3.0 is the bet that this growth is durable, not cyclical.

Zepto's Updated IPO Filing: ₹8,010 Cr Fresh Issue, July Debut in View

Zepto filed its updated DRHP with SEBI on June 8, planning a ₹8,010 crore fresh issue plus a large OFS by early investors (Nexus Ventures, Kaiser, Contrary). Co-founders Aadit Palicha and Kaivalya Vohra are not diluting their stake. FY26 revenue surged 103.6% to ₹22,624 crore; net loss widened to ₹5,905 crore. Fresh issue proceeds earmarked for ~1,900 new dark stores (₹1,629 Cr), lease payments through FY30 (₹1,735 Cr), and tech/cloud (₹1,325 Cr). Earlier: Zepto filed original DRHP in early June; IPO targeting H2 2026.

Why it matters: Zepto's IPO will be a live test of whether India's public markets can price quick-commerce growth against widening losses. A successful listing at the implied valuation (estimated ₹50,000+ Cr) sets a reference price for Blinkit, Swiggy Instamart, and BigBasket — and tests whether post-loss-widening IPOs can clear in this market.
