US and Iran Strike an Interim Peace Deal — the Strait of Hormuz Reopens After 108 Days

The US and Iran announced an initial peace agreement on June 14, with President Trump confirming the Strait of Hormuz will reopen upon signing. Iran's deputy FM Kazem Gharibabadi confirmed the deal ends hostilities immediately. Before the February blockade, roughly 25% of global seaborne oil and 20% of LNG transited the strait. The deal leaves Iran's enriched uranium stockpile and nuclear programme for a 60-day follow-on negotiation — meaning the strait could close again if those talks fail.

Why it matters: India imports ~85% of its crude oil; the Hormuz closure had driven fuel inflation to 30.33% and forced the RBI to cut its GDP forecast to 6.6%. A sustained reopening is the single largest macro tailwind India could receive — reversing the energy shock that was threatening stagflation. Earlier: 3 Indian sailors were killed in a ship strike on June 11; Brent was touching $97.

India's May Exports Hit $45.2bn — Up 18% Year-on-Year, Even as Hormuz Was Closed

India's merchandise exports grew 18% year-on-year in May 2026 to $45.2 billion, driven by petroleum products (+54.89% on high prices), engineering goods (+24.48%), and electronics (+11.62%). Combined April-May total exports (merchandise + services) reached $162.7 billion, up 14.66%. The trade deficit widened to $28.21 billion as import costs stayed elevated.

Why it matters: Strong export growth while Hormuz was still closed signals genuine diversification and underlying global demand for Indian goods. With the strait now reopening, petroleum export earnings may normalize — but engineering goods and electronics growth looks structurally sound.

Alibaba's Qwen 3.7 Max Runs 35 Hours Autonomously — and Costs 6× Less Than Claude Opus

Qwen 3.7 Max (released May 20 at Alibaba Cloud Summit) completed a verified 35-hour autonomous kernel-optimization run with 1,158 tool calls and a 10× speedup — the longest documented agentic run published. It scores 92.4 on GPQA Diamond, edging Claude Opus 4.6 Max (91.3), with a 1M-token context window. Price: $2.50 per million input tokens, roughly six times cheaper than Claude Opus 4.7. Why it matters: Frontier agentic capability at mid-tier cost changes the economics of AI-native products. Alibaba is creating real pricing pressure on US labs — and because it runs on Claude Code's harness directly, Indian developers and founders can use it today with their existing tooling.

BloombergNEF 2026 Outlook: Solar Becomes the World's Largest Power Source by 2032

BNEF's New Energy Outlook 2026 finds solar will top world electricity generation by 2032, with battery storage growing from 223 GW today to 3.8 TW by 2035 — a 17-fold jump. The Iran energy shock is credited with accelerating electrification by making energy independence economically urgent. By 2035, 11% of all generated megawatt-hours will be time-shifted via storage.

Why it matters: India's Hormuz exposure is exactly the fossil-fuel import risk BNEF says electrification resolves. This report frames India's aggressive solar and battery targets not as climate virtue but as energy security — a much more durable policy driver.

Oxford Creates a Stranger Schrödinger's Cat — New Quantum States Open Paths to Error-Resilient Computing

Oxford researchers published in Physical Review X (June 14) the first demonstration of quantum superpositions in which each component is itself a highly non-classical state — squeezed, trisqueezed, or quadsqueezed. The trapped-ion platform gives programmable control over the shape, rotation, and separation of quantum components, extending the toolkit for quantum error correction and sensing.

Why it matters: Fault-tolerant quantum computing depends on error correction, and error correction depends on the richness of available quantum state families. This is the foundational science that makes the 2030s hardware layer possible.
