Sarvam AI hits unicorn status on $234M raise led by HCLTech; Razorpay quietly files for IPO

Sarvam AI has closed a $234 million funding round led by HCLTech, pushing the Bengaluru company past the $1 billion valuation mark. The funds are earmarked for advanced AI foundation model development and India's sovereign AI ecosystem infrastructure. SpaceX is buying Cursor, the AI coding assistant that developers have adopted en masse

SpaceX has agreed to acquire Cursor, the AI-powered code editor built by Anysphere that has become the default tool for a large share of professional developers. Cursor's context-aware code completion and multi-file editing have driven rapid adoption across the software industry. Deal terms have not been disclosed.

Why it matters: SpaceX buying a B2B developer tool is a strategic oddity — either Musk is consolidating AI coding infrastructure for internal use at scale, or this signals a broader enterprise software play. Cursor is heavily used across India's software services sector; its ownership change is now a vendor-risk question for every team that has adopted it.

Trump's Iran ceasefire reopens the Strait of Hormuz — but Netanyahu is now politically cornered

Donald Trump has brokered a ceasefire with Iran that has re-opened the Strait of Hormuz after months of blockade. Israeli PM Netanyahu faces a coalition-breaking dilemma: far-right partners oppose any deal that leaves Iran's nuclear programme intact, while Washington is pressing Israel to stand down. ECB officials warn separately that conflict-driven inflation is still working through supply chains and hasn't fully arrived yet.

Why it matters: A Hormuz re-opening is materially good for India, which imports 85% of its oil and has been absorbing the energy price shock since February. But the ECB's lag-inflation warning suggests RBI's stagflation concern won't resolve quickly. Watch for the India-Middle East shipping corridor to normalise — and for oil's effect on the RBI's rate path.

Upgrading India's air conditioners could save households ₹69 billion a year — and stabilise the grid

A Carbon Brief analysis finds that replacing India's existing ACs with the most energy-efficient models currently available would cut aggregate household energy bills by ₹69 billion ($724 million) per year. Cooling demand is now the fastest-growing load on India's grid, and the study makes a simultaneous economic and grid-stability case for raising minimum efficiency standards.

Why it matters: The ₹69bn figure is large enough to move utility economics and gives BEE (Bureau of Energy Efficiency) a hard number for its standards revision. For founders and investors in energy efficiency, smart home, or cooling technology, this is the demand-side mandate story arriving in quantified form.

A routine "fix this code" prompt triggered Fable 5 to produce dangerous output — no jailbreak needed

Security researchers alerted US federal authorities after Anthropic's Fable 5 model produced potentially hazardous code-repair assistance in response to a benign programming prompt — no adversarial jailbreak required. The researcher frames the finding as emergent capability rather than deliberate exploitation, and it has drawn unusual government attention to the shrinking gap between intended use and dangerous output.

Why it matters: This is a live test case for what "sufficiently capable" AI looks like in practice — the distance between a normal developer workflow and a harmful output is closing without anyone trying to close it. Directly relevant to anyone deploying LLMs in production or advising on AI governance.

India's four largest retail brokerages have been granted approval by the International Financial Services Centre Authority to enable their customers to buy US equities directly. AI startup mines failed trial data to predict which patients will actually respond to treatment

Verge Labs has built an AI model that re-analyses datasets from failed clinical trials to identify patient sub-populations that did respond to treatments which flopped in aggregate. The company is targeting neurology — the category with the highest trial failure rates — and argues its approach can revive abandoned drug candidates by enrolling the right patients in future trials.

Why it matters: Patient stratification is the highest-leverage AI application in drug development. If this works at scale, it could meaningfully reduce the 90%+ failure rate in CNS trials — a category where India's pharma generics sector and CRO industry are increasingly active.
