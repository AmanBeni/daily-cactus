Zepto Files ₹8,010 Crore IPO Papers — India's Quick-Commerce Pioneer Heads for Public Markets

Zepto has filed its updated Draft Red Herring Prospectus with a fresh issue component of ₹8,010 crore plus an offer for sale by early investors. Founded in 2021 by then-20-year-old Stanford dropouts Aadit Palicha and Kaivalya Vohra, Zepto serves 47.97 million annual transacting users across 1,139 dark stores in 66 cities, averaging 2.33 million orders per day. Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and JM Financial are lead bankers.

Why it matters: Three years from dorm room to IPO filing makes this one of India's fastest startup-to-listing arcs. The DRHP will test whether public markets can value a high-growth, still-loss-making business at a premium — and will set the reference point for India's entire quick-commerce sector. The structural question is whether dark-store economics can justify the valuation at listing.

Exponent Energy Raises ₹200 Crore to Expand 15-Minute EV Charging Across India

Bengaluru-based Exponent Energy closed a ₹200 crore Series B2 round co-led by 360 ONE Asset and TDK Ventures, with Hitachi Ventures also participating. Total funding now stands at ~$66 million. The company's core offering is 15-minute rapid charging with a 3,000-cycle battery warranty for commercial EVs. Capital will fund expansion into new cities and new commercial vehicle segments.

Why it matters: Charging speed and reliability — not vehicle cost — is the primary EV adoption bottleneck for commercial fleet operators in India. A validated 15-minute charge claim backed by institutional investors (TDK, 360 ONE, Hitachi) is a meaningful signal that the technology works beyond demo conditions. This is the kind of infrastructure story India needs more of.

Mygate Raises ₹225 Crore, Now Manages 27,000 Gated Communities and 5 Million Residents

Community management platform Mygate closed a ₹225 crore round from Dharana Capital (first check for the fund in this category). The company manages 27,000+ gated communities and 5 million residents; revenue grew 80% YoY to ₹173.5 crore in FY25 while losses narrowed 61% to ₹15.4 crore. Target: 10 million homes.

Why it matters: Mygate is a quiet infrastructure play on India's urbanization wave — not glamorous, but with strong retention and a recurring revenue model. An 80% revenue growth + 61% loss reduction in one year is one of the cleaner improvement trajectories in Indian SaaS. The path to profitability from here is visible, which matters more than headline valuation.

Meta and Reliance to Build a Data Centre at Jamnagar — India's AI Compute Stack Gets a Major Node

Meta and Reliance Industries have announced plans to build a data centre at Jamnagar, Reliance's industrial megasite in Gujarat, which already hosts the world's largest integrated refinery complex and significant telecom infrastructure. The partnership deepens both companies' commitments to India's AI compute build-out.

Why it matters: Data centre capacity is the physical foundation for deploying AI at scale in India. Jamnagar's combination of existing power infrastructure, Reliance's land bank, and Meta's technical requirements creates a potentially large-scale compute hub. Watch who else co-locates here — hyperscale data centres tend to cluster where infrastructure already exists.
