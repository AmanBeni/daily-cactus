WORLD · INDIA

Three Indian Sailors Dead as US-Iran Ship Conflict Reaches Indian Waters

Three Indian crew members were confirmed killed off the Shinas coast of Oman on June 11 — the third vessel attack this week linked to the ongoing US-Iran conflict. Iran has effectively blockaded the Strait of Hormuz since February 28, when US and Israeli airstrikes hit Iran and its supreme leader was assassinated. Brent crude now trades above $97 per barrel and analysts are modelling $200 scenarios if the closure holds.

Why it matters: Sixty percent of India's LPG imports pass through Hormuz; around 83% of the LNG and 84% of the crude oil transiting the strait goes to Asian nations. The fuel crisis is not a Western story — it is an Indian energy security story, with LPG shortages and delayed refinery deliveries already reported. India also has the world's largest merchant seafarer workforce.

INDIA

RBI Leaves Rates Unchanged, Cuts Growth Forecast to 6.6%, and Raises Inflation Outlook — the "S" Word Surfaces

The Monetary Policy Committee held the repo rate at 5.25% for the second consecutive meeting while revising FY27 GDP growth down to 6.6% (from 6.9%) and raising the inflation projection to 5.1% (from 4.6%). Elevated global energy prices — driven by the Hormuz crisis — and supply chain constraints are cited as key spillover risks driving the revision.

Why it matters: "Stagflation" — stagnant growth plus rising prices — is now appearing in mainstream market commentary about India. That word usually lives in developed-economy discussions; its appearance here shifts the planning assumption for FY27. For anyone advising companies or building businesses in India, a higher-inflation, lower-growth environment is a fundamentally different game than the one being played 12 months ago.

CLIMATE & ENERGY

Every Major Energy Model Agrees: The 1.5°C Climate Target Is Gone

Resources for the Future's 2026 Global Energy Outlook — harmonizing 15 scenarios from eight major organizations including the IEA, BP, Shell, and ExxonMobil — concludes that limiting global warming to 1.5°C is "no longer plausible." Wind and solar more than double across all scenarios, but emissions are only forecast to peak by 2030–35, leaving a 2°C ceiling also "extremely challenging."

Why it matters: This is not one pessimistic model — it is the scientific and energy establishment's equivalent of a unanimous verdict. For India, the implication is that every long-term infrastructure, agriculture, and city-planning decision needs to be built around adaptation as a certainty, not a contingency. The bet is now on resilience alongside decarbonization.

AI

White House Sidesteps Mandatory AI Rules Again — Issues Voluntary Framework for Pre-Release Model Review

President Trump signed an AI executive order on June 2 asking AI companies to voluntarily submit their most powerful models for government review up to 30 days before public release, and directing federal agencies to build an "AI cybersecurity clearinghouse" for vulnerability coordination. The order explicitly states that nothing creates a mandatory licensing or pre-clearance requirement. Within 60 days, agencies must develop classified benchmarks to identify "covered frontier models."

Why it matters: The voluntary framing is the whole story — the US government is choosing to ask the industry nicely rather than regulate it. For AI founders and strategists watching US-EU regulatory divergence, the US is doubling down on innovation speed over governance guardrails. India's own regulatory approach, navigating between these two poles, is watching closely.

AI

OpenAI Rebuilds ChatGPT's Memory from Scratch with "Dreaming V3"

OpenAI's Dreaming V3 system, rolling out since June 4 and now reaching Free-tier users, replaces ChatGPT's earlier memory mechanism with a background synthesis engine that captures context automatically, updates stale facts (e.g., a scheduled trip that has since passed), and achieves 5× compute efficiency. Factual recall improved to 82.8%, preference adherence to 71.3%, and time-sensitive accuracy to 75.1%.

Why it matters: The jump from "ChatGPT sort of remembers you" to "ChatGPT maintains a live, auto-updating model of your context" is qualitatively different. For anyone building AI-native workflows or advising on AI tools, this is the feature most likely to change daily usage patterns — and the implicit standard other assistants will now have to match.

INDIAN STARTUPS

Zepto Files ₹8,010 Crore IPO — India's Quick-Commerce Poster Child Heads for Public Markets

Zepto has filed its updated Draft Red Herring Prospectus with a fresh issue component of ₹8,010 crore plus an offer for sale by early investors. Founded in 2021 by 21-year-old Stanford dropouts, Zepto now serves 47.97 million annual transacting users across 1,139 dark stores in 66 cities, averaging 2.33 million orders per day. Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and JM Financial are the lead bankers.

Why it matters: Three years from dorm room to IPO filing makes this one of India's fastest startup-to-listing arcs. The listing will test whether public markets accept a business that is still loss-making at serious scale — and will set the valuation reference point for India's entire quick-commerce sector. For founders and investors, this is the proof-of-concept moment for the dark store model.

Sophia Genetics and Memorial Sloan Kettering Sign AI Precision Oncology Joint Venture

Sophia Genetics and Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center signed an MOU on June 4 to form a joint venture combining MSK's 150,000+ sequenced tumor samples and linked clinical outcome data with Sophia Genetics' AI diagnostics platform (2.5 million cases analyzed since 2014). The venture aims to develop next-generation precision oncology tools for hospitals, labs, and biopharma partners globally.

Why it matters: When the world's leading cancer center pairs its clinical depth with an AI genomics platform, the combination compresses the timeline from "tumor sequenced" to "treatment recommended." The model — top-tier hospital expertise × AI scale = deployable product for the rest of the world — is the health tech formula that will define the next decade of diagnostics.
