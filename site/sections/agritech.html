Aquapulse: Solving Shrimp Farming’s Biggest Bottleneck in India

India’s aquaculture sector has long struggled with a costly problem: farmers produce high-value shrimp but much of that value is lost to disease, poor feed efficiency, and opaque intermediary chains. Aquapulse is building IoT and AI tools to help shrimp farmers improve yields and access better pricing directly.

Key takeaway: Aquapulse is tackling yield and margin loss in a $5B+ Indian shrimp export sector using real-time farm monitoring and direct market linkages.

Why it matters: Indian aquaculture is a massive, underserved sector where technology adoption is still early. This is the kind of deep-domain vertical where AI-generalist founders can build defensible advantage.

Vivici Bags €12.5M to Scale Precision-Fermented Dairy Proteins

Vivici, a precision fermentation startup producing dairy proteins without cows, secured €12.5M in EIC funding to scale its platform. The funding reflects growing European appetite for alternative protein infrastructure as conventional dairy faces both climate and welfare scrutiny.

Key takeaway: European public funding is now backing precision fermentation at commercial scale — a signal that alt-protein is moving from lab to supply chain.

Why it matters: India’s dairy sector ($100B+) faces long-term disruption risk from precision fermentation. Indian dairy co-ops and large processors need a view on this timeline now, not in five years.

EU Approves Fermotein Mycoprotein After Six-Year Process — A Signal for Alt-Protein Regulation

The Protein Brewery won EU novel foods approval for Fermotein mycoprotein after a six-year regulatory process. The GFI notes the case shows the need to ensure regulatory frameworks keep pace with food innovation.

Key takeaway: Six years from filing to EU approval for mycoprotein — the regulatory lag is the biggest bottleneck for alt-protein commercialisation in Europe.

Why it matters: For Indian agrifood startups eyeing EU market entry — especially in novel food categories — regulatory timeline is the primary strategic variable, not just technology.

AI’s Role in Agrifood Biotech Comes Into Sharper Focus

As AI transforms scientific discovery in agrifood biotech, industry players are grappling with the extent of its real-world use cases. The analysis distinguishes between AI as a research accelerant (protein design, crop genomics) and AI as a commercial operations tool — finding the former far more mature.

Key takeaway: AI is transforming agrifood R&D pipelines but commercial deployment is still catching up — the bottleneck is integration and talent, not the models themselves.

Why it matters: India’s agritech sector is at an inflection point. Companies that can bridge the AI R&D capability with on-farm implementation will capture the next wave of value.
