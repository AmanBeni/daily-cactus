Garner Health Raises $100M at $2.74B Valuation — AI Care Navigation Reaches Revenue Scale

Garner Health closed a $100M Series E on May 28, led by Index Ventures with participation from Kleiner Perkins, Sequoia, Founders Fund, and Kaiser Permanente Ventures, valuing the company at $2.74B. Garner partners with ~800 employers to help employees find high-quality healthcare providers using clinical outcome data, with clients seeing an average 12% annual reduction in healthcare spending. Gross ARR is approximately $200M, having more than doubled for five consecutive years. The newly released Garner Research Agent uses AI to automate clinical performance measurement at scale across the medical literature.

Why it matters: Healthcare navigation is unglamorous, but the numbers are remarkable: 12% cost reduction at scale means a $24M saving per $200M healthcare spend. This is the deployment story health tech needs more of — AI delivering measurable economic value in an existing system, at revenue scale, with five years of consecutive ARR doubling. The employer-funded, outcome-data-driven model is directly portable to India's growing corporate health insurance market.

Infosys-Backed 4baseCare Raises Rs 128 Crore to Scale AI Precision Oncology Across Emerging Markets

Bengaluru-based precision oncology startup 4baseCare closed a combined Rs 128 crore — an initial Rs 90 crore Series B followed by a Rs 38 crore top-up led by growX Ventures and Infosys, with participation from Ashish Kacholia and Yali Capital. The company's flagship platform OncoTwin uses clinico-genomic and real-world patient data to generate treatment insights for oncologists. 4baseCare plans to expand its genomics laboratory network across India, the Middle East, Southeast Asia, and Latin America.

Why it matters: India has a massive unmet need in cancer diagnostics — genomics-based precision oncology reaches a fraction of the patients who would benefit from it. 4baseCare's model (AI layered on genomic sequencing data at affordable cost points) is precisely how this gap gets addressed. Infosys's participation as a corporate investor signals that large Indian tech firms are backing deep health tech seriously, not just investing in SaaS wrappers.
