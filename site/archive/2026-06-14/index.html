US and Iran on the Verge of a Deal — Hormuz Could Reopen Within Days

Pakistani PM Shehbaz Sharif said on June 12 that a peace deal between the US and Iran is "closer than ever before," with Pakistan acting as mediator and an MoU signing expected imminently. Trump posted on Truth Social that "the Deal is scheduled to get signed tomorrow" — Iran's foreign ministry urged caution on the timeline but confirmed deal text had been exchanged. Under the draft terms, Iran dismantles its nuclear programme and reopens the Strait of Hormuz; the US lifts airstrikes and eases sanctions. Earlier this week: the strait has been effectively closed since February 28; a 95% drop in crude tankers has kept Brent above $97; 3 Indian sailors were killed in a ship strike June 11.

Why it matters: If the deal closes, the biggest near-term oil price shock since 2022 ends — with immediate ripple effects on India's energy import bill, CPI inflation, and the stagflation narrative that has been dogging the RBI's policy room since its June MPC meeting.

INDIA · CLIMATE

India's Carbon Market Goes Mandatory: 490 Industrial Plants Now Face Binding GHG Targets

India's Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS) now has binding compliance obligations for ~490 entities across seven energy-intensive sectors — aluminium, cement, chlor-alkali, pulp and paper, petroleum refining, petrochemicals, and textiles. Entities that outperform their emission intensity targets earn tradeable Carbon Credit Certificates; those that fall short must purchase and surrender them. First credits are expected October 2026, with initial trading from November 2026 through January 2027.

Why it matters: This is India's first binding carbon price signal — a structural shift for energy-intensive industry and a prerequisite for managing CBAM exposure in EU exports. India's industrial carbon market is now operational, not aspirational. For strategy roles at conglomerates, energy firms, or climate-focused investors, the CCTS compliance timeline is a planning reality.

South Korea's Yoon Gets 30 More Years for Ordering Military Drones Over Pyongyang

A Seoul court sentenced ousted President Yoon Suk Yeol to 30 additional years in prison on June 12, after finding him guilty of ordering South Korean military drones to fly over Pyongyang — a provocation designed, the court found, to justify his December 2024 martial law declaration. Autism Isn't One Condition: Brain Scans Identify Two Biologically Distinct Subtypes

An international team analysing fMRI data from ~2,000 individuals — including 940 autistic people — found that autism clusters into two distinct brain connectivity patterns: hypoconnectivity (reduced communication between regions, linked to synaptic dysfunction) and hyperconnectivity (elevated connectivity, linked to immune and transcriptional pathways). The finding was validated across 20 genetic mouse models. Published in Nature Neuroscience.

Why it matters: This has immediate clinical implications: one-size-fits-all autism interventions may be actively failing one of the two biological groups. It also opens the door to biomarker-based diagnosis and more targeted therapies — the kind of precision medicine approach that is reshaping how neurodevelopmental conditions are treated.
