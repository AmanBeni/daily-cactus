Three Indian Sailors Killed as Iran-Linked Ship Attacks Continue in the Arabian Sea

India confirmed on June 11 that three Indian crew members were killed near Shinas port off Oman — the third vessel attack this week linked to the ongoing US-Iran conflict. Iran has effectively blockaded the Strait of Hormuz since February 28, following US-Israeli airstrikes and the assassination of Iran's supreme leader. The IRGC has warned all commercial shipping against attempting to transit the strait.

Why it matters: The Strait of Hormuz carries roughly 20% of the world's oil supply. Each additional ship attack reinforces the economic shock already being felt in commodity prices globally — and each Indian seafarer killed makes the conflict personal for the country that supplies the world's largest merchant navy workforce. This is the most consequential ongoing global story.

FISA Section 702 — the Surveillance Authority That Stopped a Terror Plot — Is Nearing Expiration

The US foreign intelligence community's most valuable surveillance authority, FISA Section 702, is heading toward expiration after House Democrats blocked a temporary extension, citing disagreements with Trump's nomination of Bill Pulte as acting Director of National Intelligence. The program has been credited with stopping multiple terrorist plots, including a 2024 attack on a Taylor Swift concert in Vienna.

Why it matters: A potential lapse in US foreign surveillance authority — with active military operations ongoing in the Persian Gulf and heightened threat environments — would be an unusual intelligence gap at a sensitive moment. How the impasse resolves will be a test of whether US political dysfunction extends to national security functions.
