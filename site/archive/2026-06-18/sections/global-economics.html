Global Economics

Fed’s Warsh Opens With a Hold and a Warning: Hikes Are Coming

Kevin Warsh began his Fed chairmanship with a rates hold but a clear hawkish tilt. Inflation remains above target and he strongly hinted at a rate hike before year-end. He also signalled plans to revamp how the Fed communicates and operates, raising uncertainty about the institution’s future direction.

Key takeaway: Warsh’s debut confirms the Fed is moving toward rate hikes again — tightening global dollar liquidity at a moment when emerging markets are already under pressure.

Why it matters: A potential Fed hike stacked on top of BOJ at a 30-year high and a recently cut RBI creates capital outflow risk for India. This is the macro backdrop against which India’s IPO pipeline and startup valuations are being set.

90% of Economists Now Expect BOJ to Hike Again by December

An overwhelming majority of Bank of Japan watchers expect the BOJ to raise rates again by December following this week’s hike to the highest level since 1995. The hawkish BOJ adds to a globally synchronised tightening cycle that is squeezing dollar liquidity for emerging markets.

Key takeaway: The BOJ is firmly in rate-hiking mode — a December hike would take rates to their highest since 1997, with major yen appreciation implications.

Why it matters: Yen appreciation attracts capital back to Japan from EM assets. India’s FII flows and currency stability both face headwinds from a strengthening yen cycle.

Strategists Flag Yen Intervention Risk as Dollar Rallies Post-Fed

Markets are on watch for Japanese FX intervention after the Fed’s hawkish Warsh debut triggered a dollar rally and a yen fall to levels that historically trigger Japanese authorities to act. Strategists say the 155–160 range is a trigger zone.

Key takeaway: USD/JPY is back in intervention territory — watch for BOJ/MOF action that could trigger sharp EM currency moves.

Why it matters: A sudden yen spike from intervention would trigger broad EM asset sell-offs. Indian equities and the rupee would both face short-term pressure as global risk is repriced.

Indonesia and Philippines Set to Hike Rates as Asian EM Caution Prevails

Two of Asia’s most exposed central banks — Indonesia and the Philippines — are poised to tighten monetary policy further, staying on guard as inflation and currency risks persist despite the partial Middle East de-escalation.

Key takeaway: Indonesia and Philippines hiking signals Asian EM central banks are in defensive mode — India’s recent cut looks increasingly like an outlier.

Why it matters: If peer EM economies are hiking while India holds or cuts, the rupee faces differential pressure. This is the uncomfortable macro context for the RBI’s next decision.

Brazil Cuts to 14.25% But Flags Worsening Inflation — Easing Cycle May Be Near End

Brazil’s central bank cut its key rate by 25bps for a third straight meeting, but flagged a worsening inflation outlook. Many economists now bet the easing cycle is ending — a broader signal of deteriorating EM macro conditions.

Key takeaway: Brazil’s easing cycle is likely near its end despite high rates — a cautionary signal for EM policymakers who cut too fast.

Why it matters: Brazil’s experience mirrors India’s dilemma: inflation risks from the Hormuz shock argue against cutting, but growth risks argue for it. The RBI is navigating the same tension.
