EU to Sign Free Trade Agreement With India by Year-End, Von der Leyen Commits at G7

European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen publicly committed at the G7 that the EU and India will sign a free trade agreement before end of 2026. The deal would cover goods, services, intellectual property, and digital trade — ending two decades of stalled negotiations.

Key takeaway: A G7-stage commitment from the Commission President is the most credible signal yet that the India-EU FTA has a real year-end deadline.

Why it matters: Combined with India’s AI sovereignty positioning at G7 and the AI Impact Summit next week, this is India’s most significant week of global positioning in years. The FTA’s digital trade chapter could be transformative for Indian tech exports to Europe.

Modi Raises Seafarers Issue With Trump at G7 — Trump Pledges US Will Defend India

PM Modi raised the fate of Indian seafarers caught in the US-Iran conflict directly with President Trump at G7, calling it a matter of “utmost importance.” Trump responded by pledging that the US will defend India, in what observers call one of the strongest bilateral statements of the summit.

Key takeaway: Modi elevated the Indian seafarers issue to direct bilateral diplomacy with Trump — and got a public US security commitment in return.

Why it matters: India has ~240,000 seafarers globally — the world’s second-largest supplier of maritime labour. Their safety and repatriation is not just humanitarian but strategically relevant to India’s global maritime role.

Before Telegram Ban, Government Flagged Limited Search — Platform Missed Proactive Steps

Internal details of the pre-ban government meeting on Telegram reveal that officials flagged the platform’s limited search functionality as making child safety enforcement difficult. Telegram’s failure to proactively remove content — rather than just respond to takedown notices — was the core government complaint.

Key takeaway: The Telegram ban was about enforcement architecture, not political content — the government wanted proactive safety mechanisms, not just reactive takedowns.

Why it matters: This signals a hardening standard for social platforms operating in India: proactive moderation is now a compliance requirement, not optional. Any platform or app with UGC content needs to understand this shift.
