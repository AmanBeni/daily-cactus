Vetic Raises $40M to Scale Its Veterinary Clinic Network

Pet healthcare startup Vetic secured $40M (≈₹377 Cr) led by existing investor Bessemer Venture Partners to expand its clinic network across India. Vetic operates full-service veterinary clinics and is betting on rising pet ownership among urban millennials.

Key takeaway: Bessemer doubling down on Vetic validates the Indian petcare market as a genuine consumer healthcare vertical, not just a trend.

Why it matters: India’s petcare market is early but growing fast. Vetic’s model of branded, tech-enabled vet clinics mirrors what Practo did for human healthcare. This is a category-creation bet worth watching.

Gnani.ai Launches Prisma v2.5 — Doubles Down on Sovereign Voice AI

Enterprise voice AI startup Gnani.ai launched Prisma v2.5, its latest speech-to-text model. The model spans multiple Indian languages and dialects, and Gnani is positioning it explicitly as a “sovereign” AI offering — data doesn’t leave Indian servers.

Key takeaway: Gnani.ai is moving up the stack from speech recognition to sovereign voice AI infrastructure — a well-timed bet given G7-level AI sovereignty discussions.

Why it matters: India’s sovereign AI narrative just got G7-level validation. Voice AI for Indian languages is a wedge into a massive underserved market, and Gnani is ahead of most competitors on multilingual STT quality.

Amit Shah Calls for AI-Led Revamp of National Cybercrime Helpline

Union Home Minister Amit Shah directed officials to use AI to revamp the system powering the National Cybercrime Helpline, citing the growing sophistication of online fraud. The announcement signals government intent to accelerate AI adoption in public safety infrastructure.

Key takeaway: The Home Ministry directing AI integration into national cybercrime infrastructure is a significant government procurement signal for Indian AI and regtech startups.

Why it matters: Government-directed AI transformation creates procurement opportunity for Indian AI startups. Cybercrime, fraud detection, and citizen services AI are categories where Indian founders have domain and language advantages.

BRND.ME (Erstwhile Mensa Brands) Eyes IPO Within 12–18 Months After Redomicile

Ecommerce unicorn BRND.ME (formerly Mensa Brands) has redomiciled to India from Singapore following NCLT approval, and is now targeting an IPO within 12–18 months. The company aggregates and scales direct-to-consumer brands.

Key takeaway: BRND.ME’s redomicile completes a three-year process — the IPO timeline means another unicorn heading to Indian public markets within 18 months.

Why it matters: The redomicile-to-IPO playbook is becoming a template for Singapore-incorporated Indian unicorns. With India’s $5T market cap and a crowded IPO pipeline, the window is now.

Dhan Parent Raise Securities: FY26 Profit Falls 20% to ₹326 Cr, Revenue Crosses ₹900 Cr

Raise Securities, parent of stockbroking platform Dhan, saw FY26 net profit fall 20% to ₹326 Cr from ₹406 Cr, even as revenue crossed ₹900 Cr. The decline reflects margin compression as the discount broking market intensifies and SEBI tightened F&O rules.

Key takeaway: India’s discount broking boom is plateauing — revenue grows but margins compress under regulatory tightening.

Why it matters: The SEBI-driven reset in F&O trading volumes is reshaping fintech economics. Understanding how broking-adjacent fintechs (Dhan, Smallcase) adapt their models is useful for anyone building in Indian financial services.

Pramaana Labs Raises $27M to Build an AI Verification Layer

AI startup Pramaana Labs raised $27M to build a “verification layer” for AI outputs — a system that fact-checks and validates AI-generated claims before they reach end users or downstream processes.

Key takeaway: A $27M bet on AI verification signals the market will pay for trust infrastructure separately from the models.

Why it matters: “AI trust infrastructure” is emerging as a distinct category. This is a picks-and-shovels play for the enterprise AI layer that strategy operators need on their radar.
