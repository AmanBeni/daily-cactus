Modi Inaugurates Micron Semiconductor Plant in Gujarat

PM Modi inaugurated Micron Technology’s semiconductor assembly and testing facility at Sanand, Gujarat, and laid the foundation for a manufacturing unit in Uttar Pradesh — India’s most tangible chip production milestone yet.

Key takeaway: India moves from chip policy announcements to operational fab infrastructure.

Why it matters: The downstream AI hardware stack increasingly runs on supply-chain decisions made today. India’s semiconductor capacity build-out is the infrastructure layer under the AI wave.

Amazon and QuEra: Useful Quantum Error Correction by 2028

AWS and QuEra jointly committed to commercially viable quantum error correction by 2028 using neutral-atom qubits. Amazon’s AI chief separately forecast the first “commercially useful” quantum computers within 5–7 years. Multiple credible timelines are now converging on 2028.

Key takeaway: 2028 is now a credible target for error-corrected quantum, several years ahead of prior industry consensus.

Why it matters: Companies building AI systems today need to start planning cryptographic migration and quantum-ready architecture within 2–3 years, not 10. ISRO’s quantum investments look increasingly prescient.

The Secret to Marathon-Winning Humanoid Robots

On April 19, China’s Honor Lightning humanoid robot ran a half-marathon in 50 minutes 26 seconds — beating the human world record by 7 minutes and the best robot time from 2025 by almost two hours. IEEE Spectrum examines the mechanical and AI advances that made it possible.

Key takeaway: Humanoid robotics is advancing faster than most timelines assumed — the gap between robot and human physical performance is closing rapidly in controlled settings.

Why it matters: For strategy operators, humanoid robotics represents the next labour-substitution wave after software AI. India’s manufacturing ambitions need a view on when this technology enters cost-competitiveness.

Alibaba Cloud Opens France Data Centres Amid Europe’s Data Sovereignty Push

Alibaba Cloud launched data centres in France as European governments seek non-US cloud options to reduce dependency on American hyperscalers. The move comes as the G7 AI kill-switch discussion intensifies demand for sovereign cloud infrastructure.

Key takeaway: Europe’s data sovereignty anxiety is creating an opening for Chinese and other non-US cloud providers that would have been unthinkable two years ago.

Why it matters: The same sovereignty anxiety driving the EU toward India on AI is also reshaping cloud infrastructure globally. Indian cloud and AI startups have a window to position as trusted neutral alternatives.

How Musicians Can Get Paid for Training AI

As AI training datasets routinely incorporate music, a new framework is emerging for music attribution — ensuring artists get paid each time their work is used to train models, analogous to existing royalty structures for streams and radio.

Key takeaway: Music attribution for AI training is moving from advocacy to structured frameworks, signalling a broader shift in how creative IP will be compensated in the AI era.

Why it matters: India’s creative industries and content platforms (Spotify India, JioSaavn) will need to navigate these frameworks. The IP layer of the AI economy is being built now.
