G7 Leaders Sound the Alarm: They Want American AI — But Not America’s Off Switch

At the G7 summit in Evian, French President Macron and Indian PM Modi joined other world leaders in voicing a shared fear: the US could cut off access to American AI platforms without warning. The anxiety became concrete when Anthropic briefly went dark for international users earlier this month — now cited at the highest diplomatic table.

Key takeaway: Macron and Modi explicitly raised the AI kill-switch risk at G7 — making sovereign AI a headline diplomatic demand, not just a think-tank talking point.

Why it matters: This is the geopolitical context that explains why India is racing to build sovereign models (Sarvam, Gnani.ai), why states are drafting AI policies, and why the India AI Impact Summit next week is drawing world leaders. Any strategy role in Indian tech will increasingly have to answer: how dependent are we on foreign AI infrastructure, and what’s the contingency plan?

EU to Sign Free Trade Agreement With India by Year-End, Von der Leyen Commits at G7

European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen told G7 leaders the EU and India will sign a long-stalled free trade agreement before end of 2026 — covering goods, services, IP, and digital trade. If delivered, it would be the most significant bilateral deal India has signed in decades.

Key takeaway: After 20+ years of stalled talks, a G7-stage commitment from the Commission President gives the India-EU FTA a firm year-end deadline for the first time.

Why it matters: With the US retreating from multilateralism and European tech seeking non-US AI partners, this deal opens a direct services corridor for Indian founders and strategy operators. The timing alongside the AI sovereignty debate at G7 is not a coincidence.

New Fed Chair Warsh Holds Rates But Warns a Hike Is Coming Before Year-End

Kevin Warsh opened his Federal Reserve chairmanship with a rates hold and a hawkish lean — inflation is still above target and he strongly hinted at a hike before year-end. US stocks fell on the announcement. Warsh also signalled plans to revamp how the Fed communicates.

Key takeaway: Warsh’s debut confirms a pivot: the Fed is moving toward rate hikes again, tightening global dollar liquidity at a moment when emerging markets are already under pressure.

Why it matters: A Fed hike stacked on BOJ at a 30-year high and a recently cut RBI creates capital outflow risk for India. This is the macro backdrop against which India’s IPO pipeline (Zepto, Jio) and startup valuations are being set.

US-Iran Deal Published — Hormuz to Reopen, But Shippers Aren’t Rushing Back

Washington and Tehran published the official agreement to end the Iran war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Key terms: waived US oil sanctions on Iran, Lebanon provisions, a ceasefire framework. But analysts call the language deliberately vague — Maersk and major carriers are unlikely to resume Hormuz routes soon.

Key takeaway: The deal is public but weak: major shippers aren’t ready to sail through Hormuz yet, so elevated oil prices persist under a formal truce.

Why it matters: India imports ~85% of its crude through Hormuz-adjacent routes. A vague deal means the energy shock continues for weeks, keeping pressure on the RBI’s already uncomfortable inflation forecast.

PM Modi formally inaugurated Micron Technology’s semiconductor assembly and testing facility at Sanand, Gujarat, and laid the foundation for a manufacturing unit in Uttar Pradesh. These mark India’s most tangible step yet from policy to physical chip production.

Why it matters: The domestic semiconductor build-out is the infrastructure layer under the AI wave. Rajasthan’s data-centre ambitions and India’s AI push both depend on supply-chain decisions being made now.

Amazon and QuEra Promise Commercially Useful Quantum Error Correction by 2028

AWS and quantum startup QuEra jointly committed to commercially viable quantum error correction by 2028 — ahead of most industry forecasts. QuEra uses neutral-atom qubits with demonstrated error-correction advantages. Amazon’s AI chief separately forecast the first “commercially useful” quantum computers within 5–7 years.

Key takeaway: Multiple credible players are now naming 2028 for error-corrected quantum computing, compressing the AI-quantum convergence timeline by several years.

Why it matters: If 2028 is real, companies building AI systems today need to start thinking about cryptographic migration and quantum-ready architecture within 2–3 years, not 10.

India to Hit 300 GW of Non-Fossil Power by End of Quarter — Five Years Ahead of Schedule

Union Minister Pralhad Joshi announced India will cross 300 GW of installed non-fossil fuel capacity — solar, wind, hydro, and nuclear — before the end of the current quarter. India also launched a Green Hydrogen Certification Portal to bring transparency to its nascent hydrogen supply chain.

Key takeaway: India is hitting its original 2030 non-fossil fuel target roughly five years early and is now building credentialing infrastructure to export green hydrogen.

Why it matters: 300 GW reframes India’s energy security story at a moment when the Iran-Hormuz shock is still fresh. The Green Hydrogen Portal is India’s bid to become a future exporter — a market the EU-India FTA could directly accelerate.

India’s Pramaana Labs Raises $27M to Build an AI Verification Layer

Pramaana Labs raised $27 million to build a “verification layer” for AI outputs — a system that fact-checks and validates AI-generated claims before they reach end users or downstream processes. The round reflects growing enterprise concern about AI hallucinations and reliability.

Key takeaway: A $27M bet on AI verification signals that the market will pay for trust infrastructure separately from the models themselves.

Why it matters: Pramaana’s raise signals the emergence of “AI trust infrastructure” as a category — the enterprise layer that makes AI outputs safe enough to act on. This is a picks-and-shovels play that strategy operators need on their radar.
