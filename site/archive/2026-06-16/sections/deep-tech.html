Oxford Creates a Stranger Schrödinger's Cat — New Quantum States Open Paths to Error-Resilient Computing

Oxford researchers published in Physical Review X (June 14) the first demonstration of quantum superpositions in which each component is itself a highly non-classical state — squeezed, trisqueezed, or quadsqueezed. The trapped-ion platform gives programmable control over the shape, rotation, and separation of quantum components, extending the toolkit for quantum error correction and quantum sensing. Previous work had only created superpositions of ordinary coherent states.

Why it matters: Fault-tolerant quantum computing — the kind that makes drug discovery, materials simulation, and cryptography genuinely useful — depends entirely on error correction. New families of correctable quantum states are foundational infrastructure for the computing layer that AI hardware will eventually hit. This is the science layer that makes 2030s systems possible.
