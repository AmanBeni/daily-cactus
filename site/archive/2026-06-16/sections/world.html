US and Iran Strike a Peace Deal — Strait of Hormuz to Reopen After 108 Days

The US and Iran announced an initial peace agreement on June 14, with President Trump confirming the Strait of Hormuz will reopen upon signing. Iran's deputy FM Kazem Gharibabadi confirmed the deal ends hostilities immediately. Before the February blockade, 25% of global seaborne oil and 20% of LNG transited the strait. The agreement leaves Iran's enriched uranium stockpile for a 60-day follow-on negotiation — if those talks fail, the closure risk returns. An initial PBS/NPR report confirmed both sides are treating it as an interim deal, not a final peace.

Why it matters: The most consequential geopolitical development in months — with direct economic impact on India (oil imports, inflation, RBI rates), global shipping costs, and energy transition accelerants. Earlier: 3 Indian sailors killed in a ship strike June 11; Brent near $97; Hormuz blockaded since February 28.

South Korea's Yoon Gets 30 More Years — for the Pyongyang Drone Incident He Allegedly Staged

A Seoul court sentenced former President Yoon Suk Yeol to 30 years in prison on June 12 for conspiring to send military drones over Pyongyang in October 2024 — the incident Yoon allegedly staged as a pretext for his December 2024 martial law declaration. This adds to prior sentences: 5 years (January, martial law obstruction), life in prison (February, insurrection), and 7 years (April, arrest resistance). Yoon, already in custody, can appeal.

Why it matters: Democratic backsliding, executive overreach, and judicial accountability all played out across one ex-presidency in six months — and South Korea's courts have moved at remarkable speed. The contrast with how other democracies handle executive misconduct is instructive context for anyone watching global governance.

FIFA World Cup 2026 Is Underway: 48 Teams, Three Host Countries, Cape Verde Stuns Spain

The 2026 FIFA World Cup kicked off June 11 in Mexico City (Mexico vs. South Africa), the first ever 48-team edition spanning Canada, Mexico, and the US. Opening ceremonies featured Shakira and Burna Boy. Early standout: Cape Verde drew with Spain in the tournament's debut appearance for the island nation. The group stage runs through June 27; knockout rounds begin July 1.

Why it matters: The World Cup is the world's largest shared attention event — media consumption, advertising, and consumer spending shift meaningfully during tournament weeks. India has no team but enormous cricket-rival fan energy; the Cape Verde-Spain result is the early upset story worth following. Schedule on CBS Sports and Al Jazeera.
