Another NEET aspirant dies by suicide in Rajasthan as the paper-leak crisis deepens

A student identified as Umesh Mali has died by suicide in Sikar, Rajasthan, amid ongoing protests over the NEET paper leak controversy. Kerala Shigella outbreak kills 4 in June — water contamination blamed ahead of monsoon

Kerala has reported a spike in Shigella (bacterial dysentery) cases, with four deaths so far this month. Cases are concentrated in Kozhikode and Wayanad districts. The state health minister attributes the outbreak to inadequate pre-monsoon cleaning of water sources in affected areas.

Why it matters: A pre-monsoon waterborne disease spike in one of India's best-governed states is a stark reminder of infrastructure gaps that persist despite high human development indicators. Watch for spread if heavy monsoon rains hit already-compromised water sources in the coming weeks.

After public outcry, India restores the uncensored Dancing Girl in a school textbook

A new edition of an Indian school textbook had covered the bare torso of the Mohenjo-daro "Dancing Girl" figurine — a renowned Indus Valley Civilisation artefact — with dark shading. After significant public backlash over the erasure of cultural heritage, the textbook has been revised to restore the original image.

Why it matters: A small episode with a pointed lesson: curriculum gatekeeping in India is a live contest, and public pushback can still reverse it quickly. Context for anyone tracking the ongoing tension between cultural heritage and modern textbook politics.

Cracks wide enough to stand in are appearing in J&K homes and farmland — causes unclear

Residents in Jammu & Kashmir are reporting large, unexplained ground cracks damaging homes and agricultural land. Some cracks are described as wide enough for two people to stand inside. Officials have not yet confirmed whether the cause is geological subsidence, soil erosion, or seismic activity.
