Sarvam AI hits unicorn status with ₹2,000 Cr raise; Razorpay quietly files for IPO

Sarvam AI has raised $234 million in a round led by HCLTech, vaulting the Indian language-model startup past the billion-dollar mark. The funds are earmarked for advanced model development, expanded compute infrastructure, and India's "sovereign AI ecosystem." In the same news cycle, Razorpay has confidentially filed its DRHP with SEBI, marking the payments giant's long-anticipated move toward public markets.

Why it matters: Sarvam going unicorn on HCLTech money signals that large Indian IT is now betting on homegrown AI over pure US-vendor licensing — a structural shift with direct implications for enterprise AI buyers, strategy roles, and India's tech stack independence. Razorpay's DRHP adds another marquee listing to India's fintech queue, with Zepto's IPO also expected in H2 2026.

BigBasket's founding era ends: Hari Menon steps down, Amazon veteran takes the CEO chair

BigBasket cofounders Hari Menon and Vipul Parekh have stepped back from executive roles to the board, ending nearly 15 years running India's dominant online grocery platform. Amazon veteran Amit Nanda takes over as CEO. The transition comes as Tata Digital faces fierce competition from Blinkit, Zepto, and Swiggy Instamart in the quick-commerce race.

Why it matters: Founder-era exits at Tata-backed platforms signal how India's largest conglomerate is professionalising its digital bets. The quick-commerce war is now a fight between professional operators with institutional capital — the scrappy founder phase is institutionalised. Watch how Nanda positions BigBasket's differentiation against 10-minute delivery players.

India's top retail brokers get IFSCA approval to offer US stock trading

Zerodha, Groww, Angel One, and Upstox have reportedly received approval from the International Financial Services Centre Authority to offer US equity trading to Indian retail investors. The move marks a significant opening of cross-border investment access for India's massive and growing retail trading base, which now exceeds 100 million demat accounts.

Why it matters: Giving retail investors direct US equity access changes portfolio construction behaviour at scale — and raises real questions about capital flows, LRS limits, and whether Indian platforms can compete on UX and pricing against global brokers once the novelty wears off. This also tests the IFSCA's ability to deliver on its promise as India's financial services sandbox.

Turtlemint heads to IPO as losses widen 19% — insurtech bets investors care more about growth

Online insurance distribution platform Turtlemint is preparing for its stock market debut as its losses widen. Net loss rose 19% year-on-year to ₹185 crore in the most recent reported period. The company is betting that investors will focus on revenue trajectory and market position in India's underpenetrated insurance sector rather than mounting cash burn.

Why it matters: A 19% widening loss isn't inherently damning for a growth-stage company, but the key questions are what's driving it — customer acquisition costs, headcount, or structural unit economics problems — and whether the commission model is sustainable as IRDAI regulation tightens. Look at CAC, renewal rates, and loss ratio before forming a view on the IPO.

TruNativ raises $30M Series B to scale its nutrition monitoring wearable

TruNativ, which makes a nutrition monitoring wearable band, has raised $30 million in a Series B round led by OrbiMed. The company plans to expand across D2C, quick commerce, modern trade, and pharmacy channels, while also growing its B2B ingredient business for the health and wellness industry.

Why it matters: A $30M raise for an Indian health-tech hardware + SaaS hybrid in a difficult funding environment is a real signal. OrbiMed's involvement — a specialist healthcare fund — adds credibility beyond typical consumer brand investors. The B2B2C ingredient angle builds a data moat while monetising through multiple channels, which is structurally smarter than pure device plays.

YourNest Venture Capital closes Rs 400 crore Continuum Fund for early-stage Indian tech startups

YourNest Venture Capital has raised Rs 400 crore for its Continuum Fund, targeting early-stage Indian technology startups with a focus on enterprise tech, deep tech, and AI-adjacent companies. The fund represents a continuation of YourNest's B2B-first investment thesis.

Why it matters: A new ₹400 crore early-stage fund matters for founders looking for institutional capital outside the Sequoia/Accel axis. YourNest has a history of backing B2B enterprise companies that don't follow the hyper-growth consumer playbook — a segment that remains underfunded relative to its healthy unit economics profile.
