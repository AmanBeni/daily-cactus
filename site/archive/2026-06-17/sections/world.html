Trump's Iran ceasefire leaves Netanyahu politically trapped between his ally and his coalition

Donald Trump's surprise ceasefire with Iran has reopened the Strait of Hormuz but handed Israeli PM Netanyahu a domestic crisis: his far-right coalition partners are furious that Iran was rewarded without a full military defeat. Netanyahu now faces pressure from within his own government while being expected to visibly support a deal engineered by his key ally in Washington.

Why it matters: The Hormuz reopening directly affects India's oil import bill and the energy price trajectory driving domestic inflation. Watch whether Brent retreats sustainably from ~$97/bbl, and whether that gives the RBI room to shift its monetary stance. Netanyahu's political survival also affects whether any new escalation is possible.

Hungary's parliament votes to cap prime ministerial terms, effectively barring Orbán from returning to power

Hungary's new parliament, controlled by PM Péter Magyar's opposition alliance, has voted to limit the prime minister's role to a total of eight years — a constitutional change explicitly aimed at blocking Viktor Orbán from returning to power after Magyar defeated him in elections earlier this year. The vote fulfils a central campaign promise made to Hungarian voters.

Why it matters: Hungary's democratic backslide under Orbán was one of the defining stories of European institutional fragility over the past decade. Magyar's reversal — if it holds — is a data point in the ongoing argument about whether democratic institutions can self-repair after authoritarian drift. India's own institutional debates have some structural echoes worth noting.

Russian artist and Putin critic shot dead in Poland in suspected targeted killing

Robert Kuzovkov, a Russian artist living in exile who was known for his sharp political caricatures of Putin and Russian officials under the pseudonym Semyon Skrepetsky, has been shot dead in Poland. Polish authorities are treating it as a targeted attack. No group has claimed responsibility.

Why it matters: The pattern of Russian exiles and critics being killed in European countries sends a chilling signal to the broader diaspora community, regardless of the specific perpetrator. It's also a data point in the picture of how far Russian state or state-adjacent actors are willing to reach — and how European governments manage their Russian exile populations.

India restores the Mohenjo-daro 'Dancing Girl' to her original form in textbooks after backlash over the cover-up

A new NCERT school textbook had digitally covered the bare upper torso of the famous Indus Valley 'Dancing Girl' bronze figurine with dark shading, prompting significant criticism from historians, archaeologists, and cultural commentators. The authorities have now reversed the decision and restored the original image.

Why it matters: The episode — and the rapid reversal under public pressure — illustrates the cultural politics of India's textbook revision process. The NCERT curriculum has been under sustained scrutiny for choices that appear to reflect contemporary ideological concerns over historical accuracy. This is a small but pointed example of that dynamic, and the reversal suggests the guardrails still function when pushed.
