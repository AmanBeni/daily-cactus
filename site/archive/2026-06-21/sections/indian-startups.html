From Sarvam to INFLUISH — Indian Startups Raised $426 Mn This Week

Indian startups raised $426 million across 19 deals between June 15 and June 19 — a significant jump week-on-week. AI-native companies, deeptech, and consumer health dominated the deal table. Sarvam AI's large round anchored the AI cluster; INFLUISH represented creator economy expansion.

Key takeaway: $426M in one week, led by AI and deeptech — the funding market is in strong shape and has rotated decisively toward substance-over-story companies.

Why it matters: The 21% deeptech share of startup funding (up from 5% in 2023) is the structural signal. India is no longer primarily a consumer internet play — it is becoming a technology originator. This week's mix reflects that shift maturing.

India's Data Centre Pipeline Stands at 8.33 GW: Knight Frank India

India's data centre pipeline has grown to 8.33 gigawatts of planned capacity, driven by AI adoption, cloud migration, data localisation requirements, and hyperscaler expansion. India is now among the world's most attractive data centre investment destinations, with Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad, and Delhi-NCR as primary hubs.

Key takeaway: 8.33 GW of planned data centre capacity is not incremental — it represents a structural bet on India becoming a major AI compute and cloud hub for Asia.

Why it matters: Every GW of data centre capacity requires hundreds of MW of reliable power, specialist real estate, and enormous cooling infrastructure. This pipeline will reshape Indian power markets, boost industrial real estate, and create thousands of high-skill technical jobs — with Rajasthan's solar belt potentially supplying a significant share of the power.

Recykal Bags $23Mn to Take Waste Management Solutions Global

Waste management startup Recykal has raised $23 million (approximately ₹217 crore) in a bridge funding round. The company operates a digital marketplace for recyclables and waste exchange, and plans to expand its model into Southeast Asia and the Middle East with the fresh capital.

Key takeaway: Recykal's global push validates the hypothesis that India-built waste management tech can work in emerging market contexts where informal waste sector formalization is underway.

Why it matters: India generates 62 million tonnes of solid waste per year, and much of the digitisation opportunity is untapped. A globally proven model also creates leverage for better domestic policy (EPR compliance, extended producer responsibility) as Indian founders can point to international traction.

India Built a Startup Ecosystem. Now It Needs an Innovation Ecosystem.

India's startup ecosystem has matured — 22 new-age tech companies went public in FY26 — but the country still lacks the research-to-commercialisation pipeline that transforms startups into technology originators. Deeptech funding has grown from 5% to 21% of the total, yet India still imports most of its core technology and licenses its foundational IP from abroad.

Key takeaway: Going public is not the same as going deep. India's next challenge is cultivating original IP, not just deploying capital into faster copies of proven models.

Why it matters: The difference between a consumption economy with tech companies and a genuine innovation economy is R&D investment, university-industry pipelines, and patient capital for long-cycle research. India's policy on this has been aspirational rather than structural. The window to change that is narrowing as China and the US consolidate their technology leads.

Zerodha Capital Income Jumps 44%; Loan-Against-Securities Book Reaches ₹580 Crore

Zerodha's NBFC arm, Zerodha Capital (ZPCL), recorded a 44% jump in income, with its loan-against-securities portfolio expanding to ₹580 crore as of March 31, 2026. The growth reflects both rising retail wealth management activity and Zerodha's disciplined expansion into financial products adjacent to its brokerage core.

Key takeaway: Zerodha's capital arm is becoming a meaningful business in its own right — disciplined, profitable, and growing on the back of India's rising equity investor base.

Why it matters: India added ~40 million new Demat accounts in the past two years. As those accounts accumulate holdings, LAS and other securities-backed credit products will scale with them. Zerodha is best positioned to capture this flow given its trust advantage with retail investors.
